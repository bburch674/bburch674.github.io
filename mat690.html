MAT 690 - Advanced Topics in Mathematics: Statistical Linear Models

Overview

This course is a mathematical introduction to statistical linear models. The course will cover the random vectors, distribution theory, general linear models, maximum likelihood estimation, confidence intervals, hypothesis tests, and applications. We will understand how statistical linear models provides a unifying structure when responses depend on parameters or effects in a linear manner.

The lectures are very important for understanding the material, completing the homework, and doing well on the exams. Please take notes during class and ask questions if you need a topic clarified. I strongly encourage you to make use of my office hours. This is a great way to get extra help on lecture material, homework questions, and exam preparation.
