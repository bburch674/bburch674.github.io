STA 674 - Mathematical Statistics II

Overview

This course provides a rigorous mathematical treatment of the theory of statistics, probability models, random variables and vectors, and families of distributions. This includes understanding the basic concepts of probability and distribution theory, and applying these concepts to solve problems, construct proofs, and test conjectures. A famous statistician (R. A. Fisher) once said, "Statistics can be regarded as the study of populations, as the study of variations, and as the study of methods of the reduction of data." We will come to understand how this statement made in 1925 still applies today and how mathematical statistics serves as the foundation for making decisions based on data.

The lectures are very important for understanding the material, completing the homework, and doing well on the exams. Please take notes during class and ask questions if you need a topic clarified. I strongly encourage you to make use of my office hours. This is a great way to get extra help on lecture material, homework questions, and exam preparation.

A Few Textbooks on Probability and Mathematical Statistics

  • Theory of Point Estimation (2nd Edition), 2003, by E. L. Lehmann, George Casella. Springer.
  • Testing Statistical Hypotheses (3rd Edition), 2008, by E. L. Lehmann, Joseph P. Romano. Springer.
  • Introduction to the Theory of Statistics (3rd Edition), 1978, by A. M. Mood, F. A. Graybill, and D. C. Boes. McGraw-Hill.
  • Mathematical Statistics with Applications (7th Edition), 2008, by D. D. Wackerly, W. Mendenhall, and R. L. Sheaffer. Thomson Learning.
  • Mathematical Statistics with Resampling and R (2nd Edition), 2018, by L.M. Chihara and T.C. Hesterberg. Wiley.
