STA 575 - Applied Sampling

Overview

This course covers a variety of sampling designs which are used to extract information from data. This includes simple random sampling, stratified sampling, systematic sampling, cluster sampling, multistage sampling, detectability, line and point transects, and capture-recapture sampling. A famous statistician (R. A. Fisher) once said, "Statistics can be regarded as the study of populations, as the study of variations, and as the study of methods of the reduction of data." We will come to understand how this statement made in 1925 still applies today and how sampling can be used to make quality decisions.

The lectures are very important for understanding the material, completing the homework, and doing well on the exams. Please take notes during class and ask questions if you need a topic clarified.
