STA 571 - Statistical Methods II

Overview

This course covers a variety of statistical topics which are used to extract information from data. This includes the analysis of variance and covariance, multiple and partial regression, nonparametric methods, and the use of R to carry out statistical analyses. A famous statistician (R. A. Fisher) once said, "Statistics can be regarded as the study of populations, as the study of variations, and as the study of methods of the reduction of data." We will come to understand how this statement made in 1925 still applies today and why the word "population" takes on a new meaning from a statistical point of view.

The lectures are very important for understanding the material, completing the homework, and doing well on the exams. Please take notes during class and ask questions if you need a topic clarified. I strongly encourage you to make use of my office hours. This is a great way to get extra help on lecture material, homework questions, and exam preparation.

A few textbooks on statistical methods

  • Applied Linear Statistical Models (5th Edition) by M. Kutner, C. Nachtsheim, J. Neter, and W. Li, McGraw-Hill/Irwin.
  • Extending the Linear Model with R: Generalized Linear, Mixed Effects and Nonparametric Regression Models by J. Faraway, Chapman and Hall/CRC.
  • An Introduction to Statistical Methods & Data Analysis by R. Lyman Ott and Michael Longnecker, Cengage Learning.
  • The Statistical Sleuth: A Course in Methods of Data Analysis by Fred L. Ramsey and Daniel W. Schafer, Duxbury Press.
  • Biostatistical Analysis by Jerrold H. Zar, Prentice Hall.
  • Statistical Research Methods in the Life Sciences by P. V. Rao, Duxbury Press.
